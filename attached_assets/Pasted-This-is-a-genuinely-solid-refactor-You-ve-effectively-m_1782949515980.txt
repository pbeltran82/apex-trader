This is a genuinely solid refactor. You’ve effectively moved from a scripted bot to a decision pipeline, which is the correct abstraction shift.

A couple of important architectural observations before you add anything else:


🧠 What you’ve now achieved (accurately)

You now have a clean 3-stage pipeline:

1. SnapshotBuilder (data boundary)

Only responsibility: “What is the market + portfolio state?”
No trading logic contamination
This is your infrastructure layer
2. RiskEvaluator (hard constraints)

Deterministic rules only
No “opinion”, only “block / allow”
This is your safety layer
3. StrategyEvaluator (soft logic)

Produces intent, not action enforcement
Correct separation from risk (very important)
4. DecisionEngine (orchestrator)

Just sequencing + combining results
No heavy logic left inside it
This is basically the same shape used in institutional-style rule engines (simplified, but structurally correct).


⚠️ One important architectural warning (this is subtle but important)

You mentioned:

“RiskEvaluator returns list[RiskViolation]”

Good — but make sure of this constraint:

🚫 RiskEvaluator must NEVER depend on:

strategy output
market assumptions (“this looks risky”)
historical inference beyond snapshot
It must remain:

pure function of snapshot → violations

Because otherwise it becomes a second hidden strategy layer.
